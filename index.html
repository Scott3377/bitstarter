The Sangamon Valley Trail is a 5.5-mile (8.9 km) rail trail on the west side of Sangamon County in the U.S. state of Illinois. Skirting Springfield, Illinois, it extends from Centennial Park, on Springfield's southwest side, to Stuart Park on the same city's northwest side. It was constructed by a Sangamon County team in 2010-2011 at a cost of $3.8 million, and opened to the public on July 26, 2011. As of 2013, population in the trail's service area in western Sangamon County is growing rapidly.

Our goal is to raise the necessary amount of funds to initiate expansion of the trail. Funds will be directed towards planning, lobbying local officials in charge of the project, as well as materials and labor for its construction. Join us in making this expansion an expedited reality for the enjoyment of all!

FAQ

What will the money be used for?
The money will go towards planning, materials, and labor.
How soon will the project be initiated if the required funding is met?
Project initiation depends mostly on the level of public interest. In addition to the necessary funds, it would also be important to create as much awareness with local government officials as possible in order to move the issue up the priority list.
How does the bike trail benefit the community?
The bike trails provides for the following benefits:
  • Less bikers on the roads = less accidents and fewer frustrated drivers trying to get to work!
  • Escape into nature with the additional benefit of alternate mode of transportation
  • Great way to stay healthy and in shape!
